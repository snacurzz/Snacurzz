Anda Curry with Jeera Rice

Ingredients

  • For Anda Curry:
  • 4 boiled eggs, peeled
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 1/2 cup water
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • For Jeera Rice:
  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 1/2 tsp cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a pan, add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
  2. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook until raw smell disappears.
  4. Add tomato puree, turmeric, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
  5. Cook until oil separates from the masala.
  6. Add water and bring to a boil.
  7. Make small slits in boiled eggs and add them to the curry.
  8. Simmer for 10 minutes, then add garam masala and mix gently.
  9. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  10. Rinse rice until water runs clear; soak for 20 minutes.
  11. Heat ghee/oil in a pot, add cumin seeds and let them crackle.
  12. Add drained rice and sauté for 2 minutes.
  13. Add water and salt; bring to a boil.
  14. Cover, reduce heat, and cook until rice is tender and water absorbed (about 15 minutes).
  15. Fluff rice gently with a fork before serving.
